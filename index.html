Nature photography

Nature photography encompasses a wide range of photography taken outdoors and devoted to displaying natural elements such as landscapes, wildlife, plants, and close-ups of natural scenes and textures. Nature photography tends to place a stronger emphasis on the aesthetic value of the photo than other photography genres, such as photojournalism and documentary photography.

Landscape photography

Landscape photography is one of the categories of photography often associated with nature photography. It focuses on images of the natural world (such as rivers, mountains, deserts, and forests)as well as human-made structures (such as city skylines) and the interaction between the two.

Cloudscape photography

Cloudscape photography is a sub-genre of landscape photography that focuses on the sky and its cloud formations. It can be used to capture the beauty of the sky, as well as the changing weather conditions and their effects on the landscape. It can also be used to create abstract images that focus on the shapes and colors of the clouds.

Wildlife photography

Wildlife photography is a genre of photography that focuses on capturing images of animals in their natural habitats. It can include everything from close-up shots of individual animals to wide-angle shots of entire ecosystems. Wildlife photographers often spend long hours in the field, waiting for the perfect moment to capture their subjects in action.

Macro photography

Macro photography is a type of photography that focuses on capturing small subjects in great detail. It can include everything from close-up shots of insects and flowers to detailed images of textures and patterns in nature. Macro photographers often use specialized equipment, such as macro lenses and extension tubes, to achieve the high levels of magnification needed for this type of photography.
